What Gets Deleted

DocuX stores your information in two places:

  • On your device — photos, OCR text, AI tags, and document records. This data stays entirely local and is never sent anywhere unless you explicitly enable Cloud Backup.
  • In the cloud — if you are signed in with Google and have Cloud Backup enabled, your document records and account email are stored in our database (Supabase). You can delete either or both independently.

Delete Local Data on Your Device

To permanently remove all documents, scans, and app data stored on your Android device:

  1. Open Settings on your Android device.
  2. Go to Apps (or Application Manager) and find DocuX.
  3. Tap Storage & cache.
  4. Tap Clear storage.

This permanently removes all local DocuX data from your device and cannot be undone.

Delete Your Cloud Account & Backup Data

To request deletion of your account and all cloud backup data, send an email from the Google account you used to sign in to DocuX.

What to include in your request:
— The Google account email address used to sign in to DocuX
— Subject line: "Account Deletion Request"

Request Account Deletion

What Happens After a Deletion Request

  • We verify the request matches the registered account email.
  • Your cloud-backed documents, account record, and all associated data are permanently deleted from our database.
  • Deletion requests are processed within 30 days of receipt.
  • This does not affect data stored locally on your device — to remove that, follow the steps above.

Data Retained After Deletion

We do not retain personal data after a confirmed deletion request, except where required by law. For example, financial transaction records related to subscription payments are processed through Google Play and retained by Google per their own data retention policies — not by DocuX.
